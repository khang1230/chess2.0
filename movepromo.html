Pawn Promotion

Promoting a pawn is one of the main goals of chess during an endgame. The mere threat of promoting can be a significant advantage for the player who is familiar with this strategy. Here is all you need to know about pawn promotion. Pawn promotion occurs when a pawn reaches the farthest rank from its original squareâ€”the eighth rank for White and first rank for Black. When this happens, the player can replace the pawn for a queen, a rook, a bishop, or a knight. Most of the time, players promote a pawn to a queen, which is popularly known as "queening the pawn." After one player promotes a pawn, the other player has to move (unless they are checkmated, of course).
